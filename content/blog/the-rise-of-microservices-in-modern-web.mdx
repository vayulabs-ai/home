# Small Services, Big Impact

Microservices are more than an architectural pattern — they’re a way of organizing teams and responsibilities. When done right, they let teams move independently, reduce blast radius, and enable incremental rewrites.

But microservices also bring trade-offs: operational complexity, distributed debugging, and the need for strong contracts.

---

## When to Split

Splitting a monolith should follow clear signals:

- Development velocity stalls due to code churn
- Releases are blocked by unrelated changes
- Different scaling characteristics for different modules

If you don’t have these problems, the simplest architecture often wins.

---

## Observability and Ops

The success of a microservices strategy depends on observability. Distributed tracing, structured logs, and lightweight dashboards are essential. Treat operations as a first-class product.

---

## Final Thought

Design with clear APIs, invest in automation, and prioritize team boundaries over technical purity. Microservices reward discipline more than clever design.
